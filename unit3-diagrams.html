3. Causes of the Cod Collapse

Overfishing

Government & scientists overestimated the number of fish available each year. Not enough fish reached maturity to reproduce.

Improved Fishing Technology

Sonar, radar & satellite navigation, plus larger, more powerful boats, meant dramatically more fish were caught.

Uncontrolled Foreign Fishing

Trawlers from Russia and Japan caught more than was sustainable. Changed after 1977, but Canada continued to overfish.

Destructive Fishing Practices

Unwanted fish (bycatch) were thrown overboard dead. Only intended fish were reported, hiding the true scale of catch.

Changes in Natural Conditions

Ocean temperatures and salinity levels changed. Fish may have altered migratory patterns to avoid unfavourable areas.

9. Canada Growing Degree-Days (GDDs) Above 6C

Temperature and moisture are critical for agriculture. GDDs measure accumulated heat above 6C crops need a minimum threshold to grow.

Growing Degree-Days (GDDs) above 6C: 1925 (hottest) 1375 825 550 < 550 (too cold)
1925 GDD Hottest
Southern BC interior, Windsor (ON). Best for corn, soybeans, tender fruits.
1375 GDD
BC coast, southern prairies, parts of Maritimes. Good for wheat, canola, vegetables.
825 GDD
Prairie belt, St. Lawrence valley. Main grain farming region of Canada.
550 GDD Marginal
Northern edges of agriculture. Short growing season; limited crop options.
< 550 GDD Too Cold
Most of northern Canada, arctic. Agriculture not viable without greenhouses.
